    Digital History

    Digital history is the use of digital tools, technologies, and methods to research, analyze, present, and preserve historical data and narratives. It involves leveraging digital resources like databases, mapping tools, and multimedia to explore historical questions, create interactive experiences, and make history more accessible to diverse audiences. Public history is the practice of bringing history to life for a wide audience outside traditional academic settings. It focuses on sharing, preserving, and interpreting the past through museums, archives, media, historic sites, and community projects.

    Digital public history combines digital tools with public history practices to engage audiences and share historical knowledge online. It uses technology like websites, social media, virtual exhibits, and interactive platforms to make history accessible, collaborative, and dynamic.

    As a student of history; I aim to use my skills as a developer to reshape how we engage with historical narratives. My goal is not just to record history but to leverage technology to amplify diverse perspectives and challenge established theories through widespread internet access. In today’s fast-paced digital environment, the internet accelerates the questioning of traditional norms, creating opportunities for more inclusive storytelling. I’m passionate about digital history because I believe everyone deserves a voice in shaping our collective story. Through full-stack development, I want to ensure that historical events go beyond fleeting moments on social media, preserved in dynamic and enduring data structures that stand the test of time.
